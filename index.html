Learning Experience Designer for Higher Education

Hi, I’m Carrie. I design structured course improvement systems that move educators from course review to clear, evidence-based redesign decisions.

My work focuses on helping faculty and instructional designers identify what is working, diagnose barriers, and strengthen course structure. I then support high-impact improvements without lowering academic rigor.

Grounded in real course review and redesign work.

What this work supports

Course review and quality evaluation

Analyze course artifacts using evidence-based criteria aligned with Quality Matters, OLC-aligned review, and institution-specific standards.

Barrier identification and learner access

Identify where course design limits participation and distinguish meaningful rigor from avoidable friction.

Course architecture and alignment

Evaluate structure, navigation, pacing, and alignment to improve clarity and support consistent learner progress.

Targeted redesign and improvement planning

Prioritize high-impact changes and develop clear, defensible redesign recommendations for faculty and design teams.

Review → Diagnose → Structure → Redesign

How this system is used

This process is designed to support real course review and redesign work in higher education.

This process supports course review work alongside nationally recognized frameworks such as Quality Matters and OLC-aligned review, as well as institution-specific quality standards.

  • Identify observable course design issues using artifact-based evidence
  • Evaluate course quality across alignment, clarity, access, and support
  • Prioritize improvements based on learning impact
  • Develop clear, faculty-facing redesign recommendations

Each stage builds toward a final outcome: a focused, evidence-based course improvement plan grounded in instructional reasoning.

This system is designed to support consistent, evidence-based course improvement across instructional contexts.
