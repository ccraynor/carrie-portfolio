How this system is used

This process is designed to support real course design, review, and improvement work in higher education.

This process supports course review alongside nationally recognized frameworks such as Quality Matters and OLC-aligned review, as well as institution-specific quality standards.

The same approach also supports new course design by helping teams make clear decisions about structure, alignment, learner support, and quality from the beginning.

  • Identify observable course design issues using artifact-based evidence
  • Evaluate course quality across alignment, clarity, access, and support
  • Prioritize improvements based on learning impact
  • Develop clear, faculty-facing design and redesign recommendations

Each stage builds toward a final outcome: a focused, evidence-based course design or improvement plan grounded in instructional reasoning.

This system is designed to support consistent, evidence-based instructional design across course creation, course improvement, and quality review contexts.
